Poultry farming is a cornerstone of Nigeria’s agricultural sector, providing livelihoods for thousands of farmers and contributing significantly to food security. However, broiler and layer farmers face a persistent challenge: high chick mortality rates during the brooding phase. Chicks are highly sensitive to environmental conditions, and even slight fluctuations in temperature, humidity, or air quality can lead to devastating losses. For over 600 poultry farmers across Nigeria, this issue translates into reduced productivity, increased costs, and missed opportunities for growth.

The root of the problem lies in the lack of real-time monitoring and control in brooder houses. Many farmers rely on manual methods to check conditions, which are often inaccurate, time-consuming, and reactive. By the time a problem is detected, it’s often too late to save the chicks. This not only impacts the farmers’ income but also undermines the sustainability of Nigeria’s poultry industry. Without a reliable solution, these challenges will continue to hinder the sector’s potential

Enter Poultrix, an IoT-powered solution designed to transform poultry farming. Our system uses smart sensors to monitor critical brooding conditions—such as temperature, humidity, and air quality—in real-time. Farmers receive instant alerts if conditions deviate from the optimal range, allowing them to take immediate action. But Poultrix doesn’t stop there: our AI-driven platform automates climate control, adjusting heating, cooling, and ventilation systems to maintain the perfect environment for chicks. This hands-free approach not only reduces manual labor but also ensures 24/7 precision and reliability.
